Detailed Description

The base class of all connections classes.

/

In graph-based networks, vertices are connected through edges where messages are exchanged. The strength of connections is characterized by edge's weight. The connections classes define topologies, the way to connect vertices,
and dynamics, the way to change connections as time elapses, of the networks.

Connections can be either static or dynamic. The static connectons are ones where connections are established at initialization and never change. The dynamic connections can be changed as the networks evolve, so in the dynamic networks' edges will be created, deleted, or their weight will be modifed.

Connections classes may maintain intra-epoch states of connections in the network. This includes history and parameters that inform how new connections are made during growth. Therefore, connections classes will have customized recorder classes, and provide a function to create the recorder class.
